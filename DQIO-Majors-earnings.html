Visualising earning based on college majors¶

This is a guided project from Dataquest.io, where we will explore using panda's wrapper for matplotlib to express data into visual charts and graphs for easier analysis.

The dataset is a subset obtained from https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/acs/ and is cleaned and released by FiveThirtyEight on their github ( https://github.com/fivethirtyeight/data/tree/master/college-majors ).

Plotting in pandas¶

Plotting is done using dataFrame.plot() function, and passing x and y values to specify which columns you are looking to plot on. Specifying the kind='' in the function will allow you to specify the type of graph you would like to create.

Options are: line, bar, barh, hist, box, kde, density, area, pie, scatter, hexbin

Question Columns Analysis
Do students in more popular majors make more money? Sample Size vs Median Does not appear so
Do students that majored in subjects that were majority female make more money? ShareWomen vs median On the contrary. Large share of women on the course has a trend of lower salaries in employment
Is there any link between number of full-time employees and median salary? Full-time vs median Appears to have a slight negative trend. The more people from a course who become full-time employee's the slightly lower salary is earned

Share of women on a course corrolates with lower salaries - why?¶

We live in a world where high salaries are generally linked to roles that work at employers who's major concern is delivering financial results to shareholders.

However looking at the type of courses where women are the majority (in my sample table I've used 0.8+) of the student population, these roles mostly appear to be health and education driven.

If we were to do a study of roles who's aims are to educate, protect species (human or otherwise), environmental studies or just generally doing good for the world rather than a business share holder, we often see salaries drop.
